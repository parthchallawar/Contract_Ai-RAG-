Commercial Services & Licensing Agreement

This Commercial Services & Licensing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of October 24, 2024, by and between Stellar Systems Corp. ("Provider") and Meridian Enterprises ("Client").

1. Scope of Services & License Grant

Provider shall provide the commercial services defined in the applicable Statement of Work. Subject to the terms of this Agreement, Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the proprietary software platform for its internal business operations during the Term.

2. Intellectual Property Ownership

Provider retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Licensed Software. All deliverables developed under this Agreement that are specifically identified as "Work Product" shall be owned by Client upon full payment. Provider grants Client a perpetual license to use any pre-existing Provider IP embedded in such Work Product.

3. Termination for Convenience info

Either party may terminate this Agreement or any Statement of Work at any time, for any reason or no reason, upon providing sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the other party. Upon such termination, Client shall pay Provider for all services performed up to the effective date of termination.

4. Limitation of Liability warning

TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY LAW, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES. EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, THE TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY SHALL BE LIMITED TO THE TOTAL FEES PAID IN THE SIX (6) MONTHS PRIOR TO THE EVENT.

5. Indemnification security

Provider shall indemnify and defend Client against any third-party claims alleging that the Software infringes any patent, copyright, or trademark. Client shall indemnify Provider against claims arising from Client's unauthorized use of the Software or breach of third-party data privacy.

6. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County.
